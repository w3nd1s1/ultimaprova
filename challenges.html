Challenges

Throughout the development of this project, several methodological and technical challenges emerged. These involved understanding the ArCo ontology, identifying meaningful knowledge gaps, validating the generated information, and interacting with the SPARQL endpoint.

Understanding the ArCo Ontology

One of the main challenges was understanding the structure of the ArCo ontology. Although ArCo provides extensive documentation, identifying the most appropriate classes and properties for representing the missing information was not always straightforward. Several concepts that initially appeared intuitive were modeled differently within the ontology, requiring careful analysis before designing the proposed semantic enrichments.

Identifying Knowledge Gaps

Another challenge was identifying meaningful knowledge gaps. Since the ArCo Knowledge Graph already contains a considerable amount of information about Villa Lante, it was necessary to distinguish between data that were already available and information that could genuinely enrich the existing description. This required comparing the knowledge graph with reliable external sources and selecting only relevant information for the enrichment process.

Generating RDF Triples

Transforming textual information into RDF triples required careful modelling decisions. The information generated by the Large Language Models had to be translated into subject–predicate–object statements while remaining compatible with the structure adopted by ArCo. This process involved selecting appropriate resources, reusing existing identifiers whenever possible, and ensuring that the proposed triples were logically consistent and suitable for semantic integration.

Prompt Engineering

The use of Large Language Models introduced additional challenges. Different prompting strategies often produced responses with different levels of detail, structure, and technical depth. Although both ChatGPT and Gemini generated relevant information, their outputs required manual evaluation to identify the most useful content for semantic enrichment.

Verifying Information

Since part of the information was generated by Large Language Models, every statement had to be verified using reliable institutional and academic sources. This validation process was essential to ensure the accuracy of the proposed enrichments and to avoid introducing unsupported or incorrect information into the knowledge graph.

Working with the SPARQL Endpoint

Interacting with the ArCo SPARQL endpoint represented another important challenge. Some classes and properties described in the ArCo ontology are not always instantiated in the published dataset, making it difficult to formulate effective SPARQL queries. Consequently, several queries required multiple refinements before producing the expected results, highlighting the importance of understanding both the ontology and the actual structure of the available data.

Conclusion

Despite these challenges, the project demonstrated that Large Language Models can effectively support the semantic enrichment of cultural heritage knowledge graphs. However, human supervision remains essential for selecting reliable information, validating the generated content, and ensuring consistency with the ontology adopted by ArCo.
